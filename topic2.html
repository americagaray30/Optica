TOPIC 2

Eder Geovanni Ramirez Corona

Code Stage.

In this stage, the tasks commonly known as programming are performed; which essentially consists of bringing to source code, in the chosen programming language, everything designed in the previous phase, making use of prototypes as well as tests and trials to correct errors. This task is carried out by the programmer, completely following the guidelines imposed in the design and always considering the functional and non-functional requirements.

Testing stage.

In this stage, the focus is on the internal logic of the software, ensuring that all the statements have been tested, and on the external functions, tests are being carried out to ensure that the defined input will produce the results that are really required.

Integration stage.

Software integration and testing (of programs and procedures) is performed at this stage to determine if the proposed design meets the initial set of objectives for the software. Testing may be repeated, specifically to check for errors and ensure successful completion of the software

Implementation stage.

During this phase, the installation of the software is carried out, as well as the customization and different tests that can be monitored by the technical personnel responsible for the software. That is, put the software into operation, so you have to plan the environment taking into account the dependencies between its different components. There may be components that work fine on their own, but when combined they cause problems. Therefore, it is necessary to use known combinations that do not cause compatibility problems.

Operations and maintenance stage.

This is one of the most important phases of the software development life cycle. Since the software neither breaks nor wears out with use, its maintenance includes three different points:
» Eliminate defects detected during their useful life (corrective maintenance).
» Adapt it to new needs (adaptive maintenance).
» Add new functionalities (perfective maintenance).
In addition to ensuring that the software meets the requirements requested prior to design, a maintenance and support service is offered to users to ensure that the software continues to function properly.

Disposition stage.

This stage includes the end of the life cycle of the software and its withdrawal from operation. Some systematic steps must be followed to finalize the system in a secure environment that allows all useful or sensitive information of the developed software to be preserved.
