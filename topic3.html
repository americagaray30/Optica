What are static software tests?

Static tests.
They are the type of tests that are performed without running the application code. It can refer to document review, since a code execution is not done. This is because "desktop testing" can be done with the goal of following the application flows.



Technical Reviews

Two strategies can be applied to identify defects in software products, technical reviews and software testing. While the former can be applied from early stages of the software development process, including from the specification of requirements, the latter require that the software code exist to execute it and show the defects that the applied test cases could have detected.



Informal reviews

They are characterized by not having a documented process, the objective of the meeting is not focused on the detection of faults and, in general, they are carried out improvised. Therefore, it is difficult to determine its effectiveness, in terms of the number of defects found (Laporte and April, 2018).



Inspection.

The purpose of the inspection is to detect and identify anomalies in the software product, including errors and deviations from standards and specifications. It is a formal process in which the roles of each of the participants (from 3 to 6 software developers) are defined, where a trained moderator is in charge of conducting the face-to-face session.



What is a guided review?

Step-by-step presentation of a document by the author in order to gather information and establish a common understanding of its content.
